BSMT/ MLS 2 Students Immerse in Real-World Clinical and Information Systems Through Hospital Visits

BSMT/MLS 2 students from Sets 1B and 2B, under the supervision of their instructor Mr. Jhon Mario Caliguiran, RMT, conducted hospital visits as part of their Health Information System (HIS) course requirement.

The visits were held at Raphael General Hospital on July 2–3, 2025, and at Dr. Ronald P. Guzman Medical Center (RPGMC) on July 16–17, 2025.

During the visits, students were given the opportunity to observe and participate in actual laboratory processes and explore the use of Hospital Information System 8 (HIS , a widely used software in many healthcare facilities. HIS 8 integrates data from different departments such as laboratory, billing, and patient records, ensuring efficient and accurate hospital operations.

Students, under the guidance of licensed Medical Technologists and interns from MCNP and CSU, performed various laboratory tasks including pipetting, blood extraction, and processing tests such as ALT, AST, CBC, and HBsAg.

They also experienced charging and rendering requisitions using the hospital system and operated laboratory machines like the Chemistry and Hematology Analyzer.

Both hospitals provided a welcoming and accommodating environment, allowing students to ask questions and gain hands-on experience.

The activity allowed the students to apply their classroom knowledge in a real clinical setting, preparing them for their future internships and careers in the medical laboratory field.
