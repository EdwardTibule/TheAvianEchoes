USLT Holds 9th Pinning, Candle Lighting, and White Coating Ceremony for Medical Laboratory Science Interns

Saint Teresa of Calcutta Batch Embraces the Call to Accuracy, Precision, and Compassion The School of Health and Allied Sciences of the University of Saint Louis Tuguegarao proudly held its 9th Medical Laboratory Science Pinning, Candle Lighting, and White Coating Ceremony for the Saint Teresa of Calcutta Batch on July 8, 2025. With the theme “Louisian Medical Laboratory Scientists of Tomorrow: Illuminating the Path to Mission and Excellence with Accuracy, Precision, and Compassion,” the event served as a profound milestone in the professional journey of 59 aspiring interns.

The ceremony began with a Eucharistic Celebration presided over by Rev. Fr. John Mark Barroga, CICM, University Chaplain, offering a spiritual foundation for the day’s solemn rites.

The program officially opened with the processional of the BSMT/BSMLS IV students, accompanied by faculty and university administrators. An audio-visual presentation led the community into prayer, followed by a moving performance of the Acts of Filipinism by the BS in Criminology students, showcasing the importance of cultural identity and patriotism.

The symbolic portion of the event began with Ms. Melanie Joy Arellano, RMT, MT/MLS Faculty Member, who shared the symbolic meaning of the candle, describing its flame as a beacon of commitment, compassion, and guidance in the healthcare profession. The candle lighting ceremony was led with reverence by Dr. Luisa Aquino, Vp Acad, and Dr. Dindo V. Asuncion, Dean of the School of Health and Allied Sciences.

The program continued with the reading of the symbolic meaning of the departmental pins and coat, signifying the formal transition of students into the clinical phase of their professional journey.

The highlight of the event—the presentation, pinning, and coating of the candidates—was spearheaded by Mr. Alvin A. Aldea, RMT, MSc, Department Head of the MT/MLS program. He was joined by Clinical Internship Coordinators, Ms. Vivian Kristel C. Licayu, RMT, and Ms. Almira Lyka B. Ruiz, RMT, along with faculty members Ms. Arellano and Ms. Clarisse Althea A. Adduru, RMT, all of whom played instrumental roles in the academic and clinical formation of the interns.
