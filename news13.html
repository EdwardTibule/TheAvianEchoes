4th Year BS Pharmacy Students Begin Public Health and Community Internship

86 fourth-year Medical Technology Interns from Batch Saint Jude Thaddeus of the School of Health and Allied Sciences (SHAS) underwent their Two-Day Comprehensive 2 Examination last July 3 and 4, 2025.

The comprehensive exam serves as a crucial benchmark in the interns' journey, gauging their mastery of theoretical foundations before advancing further into the clinical phase of their training.

On Day 1, interns were tested on Molecular Biology, Histopathology, and Hematology 1 and 2—subjects that demand both sharp memory and strong analytical skills.

Day 2 focused on the practical pillars of laboratory diagnostics: Bloodbank, Immunology and Serology, and Automation.

Guiding the examination process were proctors Ms. Vivian Kristel Licayu, RMT and Ms. Almira Lyka Ruiz, RMT, whose presence ensured that exam protocols were strictly followed and that the testing environment remained conducive to focused performance.

As Batch Saint Jude Thaddeus closes this chapter of their academic journey, the SHAS community stands in full support of their continued growth, celebrating their perseverance and commitment to excellence in the field of Medical Technology.
